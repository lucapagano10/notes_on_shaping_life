# On Time


The most rewarding things in life take years. Only bad things happen quickly. 
Learning something new, changing old behaviors, building real relationships. All of it takes time. 

There's a concept I think about constantly: low time preference. Choosing tomorrow over today. Delayed gratification, not as a single hard decision, but as an orientation to your whole life. I think it's one of the most powerful concepts there is. Focus helps, but this is the foundation.

Think about the real leverage you have in life. Almost all of it comes from long-term work. This book is a good example. If I thought about it short-term, what it adds to any single day, it's worth nothing. But I look back at the end of the year and I have a full year of thoughts and notes. Low time preference plus focus is a superpower. People with low time preference play long-term games with long-term people. They let compounding do the work. They're not fighting against time. They're using it.

Warren Buffett once said something about his investing strategy that stuck with me. He said that his strategy in investing is very simple, anyone could copy it. The thing is, it requires accepting that you'll get rich slowly, and nobody wants that.

The strategy is available to everyone. The patience is available to almost no one. Success is a lagging indicator, you don't rise to the level of your goals, you fall to the level of your systems. The results you see today were caused by systems you built years ago. And the systems you build today won't show up until later. This delay fools everyone, it what makes people quit before the lag catches up. They never see what would have happened if they'd stayed. 
We overestimate what we can do short term and underestimate what we can do long term. 
Just the work, day after day, and learn to enjoy the process. Because you're going to spend way more time on the journey than in those brief moments of triumph at the end. 
Rockets use most of their fuel in the first minute of flight to escape the pull of gravity. Once they get outside that pull, the rest is effortless, same with the things we start. The beginning is where it costs the most, after that, momentum carries you. Things in life compound, but only if you stay. Suddenly, then all at once. That's how everything that matters arrives.
