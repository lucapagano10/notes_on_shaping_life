# On Simplicity


When in doubt, subtract. Perfection is not when there's nothing left to add. It's when there's nothing left to remove. 
Most complexity is unnecessary. We manage it instead of eliminating it because deletion takes a kind of courage that addition doesn't. Adding feels like progress, while removing feels like loss. But removing is usually the right move. 
Complex things collapse under their own weight. Companies with processes so complicated they end up forgotten. Habit trackers and routines so elaborate they're easy to forget and impossible to maintain. 
Things that aren't simple invite for excuses. Want to really procrastinate something? That’s easy, just make it complex and you’ll invent a thousand different excuses everyday.
The key to simplicity is that starting should feel obvious, maintaining should be the default, and building should happen step by step. Momentum after that is your best friend. 
Constantly ask yourself, what would this look like if it were easy? We have this instinct to complicate things. To add steps. To make it harder than it needs to be. But usually the elegant solution is simpler, not more complex. 
Also, to make every detail perfect, you need to limit the number of details. You can't perfect a hundred things. You can perfect a few. 
Complexity is is avoiding the hard decision about what actually matters. Simplicity forces clarity. Simplify until it hurts, then simplify more.
