# On Ownership


Full ownership means no excuses. A hard truth I learned is: whoever you blame has power over you. If it's their fault, you wait for them to fix it. If it's the world's fault, you wait for the world to change.

But if it's your fault, all of it, even the parts that technically aren't, then you can move. This isn't about truth. It's about what’s useful, and everything you put on other people’s behalf, has power on you. To have power and put yourself on a position where you can actually improve life, the key lies on having ownership about everything that happens to you.

Remember, no excuses. An excuse is just a story about why you couldn't, it changes nothing besides making the failure more comfortable to sit with.

Remember also, no complaints. Complaining feels like you're doing something. But it's the opposite. Every complaint is basically a confession: I've decided I can't change this. We love to complain because it's easy. It gives a tiny fraction of the satisfaction at a tiny fraction of the effort. 

A great reason for failing won't save one job. Won't gain one customer. Won't change one outcome. The energy you spend explaining your situation would be better spent finding the way out. Nothing good ever came from feeling like a victim.

Everything is within me. I own everything. When something bad happens, don't ask why it happened to you. Ask what it's for. Does the world happen to you, or do you happen to the world? Spend zero time on what you could have done. Spend all your time on what you might do. 
The victim waits, the owner moves.
