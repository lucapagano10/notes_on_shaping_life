# On Commitment


You can do anything in life, but not everything. Optionality sounds so reasonable. Keep your options open, stay flexible, don't commit too soon. So you float. You dabble. You keep every door open while walking through none of them. Optionality promises freedom, but it delivers paralysis. Every door you refuse to close is a door you don't walk through. The more options you keep, the less you choose. And the less you choose, the less you become.

Here's the cruel part. Those doors close anyway. They just close on you instead of behind you. Options have expiration dates, and often, uncommitted possibilities rot.

If something isn't a clear yes, it's a no. Nothing in between. The person who commits fully to one path goes further than the person who hedges across three. Not because they chose better. Because they chose at all. 
And remember the chapter about action, commitments are not a one way street, just choose, learn, and choose again.

The sacrifice is what makes commitment mean something. Easy yeses are dangerous, I think about focus like juggling. If you juggle too many balls, some are going to drop. The mistake is thinking you get to choose which ones.

Your capacity for excellence is inversely proportional to the number of your commitments. You have so many ideas. More than enough to kill your focus. The danger is never running out of things to do. The danger is never going deep enough on any of them. When you stop treating your current opportunity as a stepping stone to something else and start treating it as the only one that matters, opportunity finds you. 
Freedom is not infinite options, freedom is the option of choosing going all the way.
