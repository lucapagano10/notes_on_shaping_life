# On Tradeoffs


There is always a tradeoff in every decision. If you can't find it, look better. Every yes is a no to something else. Every hour spent here is an hour not spent there. Every path you walk down means a hundred paths you didn't take. And the dangerous part isn't the tradeoff itself, it's not seeing it. The worst decisions I've made weren't bold bets that went wrong. They were decisions where I didn't see the cost until it was too late. I said yes to something without realizing what I was saying no to. I chased one thing and only afterwards noticed what I'd let go of. The person who avoids hard tradeoffs doesn't avoid the cost. They just pay it later, with interest. The person who stares the tradeoff in the face, sees it clearly, and chooses anyway, is the one who ends up where they want to be.

Pick things where you understand the tradeoffs. And don't pretend you're not giving anything up, because that's when it catches you. 
It's always better to be effective, choosing the right things, than to be efficient, doing them perfectly. Because efficiency on the wrong thing is the most elegant way to waste your life. Every decision has a price, aim to know the price before buying into it.
