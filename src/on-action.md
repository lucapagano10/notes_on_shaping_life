# On Action


To begin, begin. Don’t plan to begin, nor prepare to begin. Just begin. 
Most things don't require readiness, it’s actually the other way around. Beginning is what makes you ready. You learn what you need by starting. You figure it out by doing it badly first. Overthinking is just underdoing with better vocabulary.

If more information was the answer, we'd all be billionaires with perfect abs. Trust me, we don't need more information. 
People say they're not doing the work because it's hard. But it's hard because they're not doing the work. Most people die with their best work still inside them. Ideas they never tried, words they never said. Things they kept meaning to make, but the timing was never quite right. The conditions were never right.

To procrastinate is arrogant. It assumes there will be a later, and even worse, it assumes you'll have the discipline to do it then. If you can't do it now when the impulse is fresh, what makes you think future you will be any better?

Here's what actually works to push yourself into defaulting to action: make many small bets. Try things that won't destroy you if they fail. You don't need perfect aim. You need volume and patience. Think like a venture capitalist with your own life. The secret is in creating asymmetry for yourself. Small, survivable downsides. Large, uncapped upsides. Take those bets. Take a lot of them. Most of what you try will flop. Hardly anyone will notice. A few things will take off. That's just the game.

Instead of trying to be right, make being wrong cheap. When you can't afford a mistake, it's hard to move. You paralyze, because the cost of failing is too high. So make mistakes affordable, set it up so that when something fails, it's not catastrophic. It's just another data point of learning for your next action. Get every idea out of your head and into the world. Not because every idea is good. But because the good ones and the bad ones are all tangled up together. The only way to find the good ones is to get them all out.

Focus on making things rather than consuming them. The ratio matters. Most people spend ninety percent of their time taking in other people's work and ten percent making their own. Flip it, even a little, and everything changes.

One more important thing on this, you need to put stuff out there. Make it real first, make it good later. You can improve something bad. You cannot improve something that doesn't exist. And also, the best ideas come from action. Creativity is a strange coin: the more you spend, the more you have. 
The gap between you and people who've made things isn't talent. It's that they've erred on the side of action. 
The person who acts, learns. The person who waits, wonders.
