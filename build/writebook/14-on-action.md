# On Action

To begin, begin. Not to plan to begin. Not to prepare to begin. Just begin. Most things don't require readiness. They create it. You learn what you need by starting. You figure it out by doing it badly first. Overthinking is just underdoing with better vocabulary.

final- notes on shaping life                                                                          7

If more information was the answer, we'd all be billionaires with perfect abs. We don't need more information. We need to act on what we already know. People say they're not doing the work because it's hard. But it's hard because they're not doing the work. To procrastinate is arrogant. It assumes there will be a later. And worse, it assumes you'll have the discipline to do it then. You won't. If you can't do it now when the impulse is fresh, what makes you think future you will be any better? Here's what actually works. Make many small bets. Try things that won't destroy you if they fail. You don't need perfect aim. You need volume and patience. Think like a venture capitalist with your own life. You're going to lose on nine attempts. But you'll win big on the tenth. And the wins erase the losses. The secret is asymmetry. Small, survivable downsides. Large, uncapped upsides. Take those bets. Take a lot of them. Most of what you try will flop. Hardly anyone will notice. A few things will take off. That's just the game. Think of your life like a book. Nobody remembers the weak pages. They only remember the lines that struck them. A few good ideas redeem a hundred forgettable ones. Same with your attempts. The failures fade. The wins stay. Instead of trying to be right, make being wrong cheap. When you can't afford a mistake, it's hard to move. So make mistakes affordable. Set it up so that when something fails, it's not catastrophic. It's just data. Make it real first. Make it good later. You can improve something bad. You cannot improve something that doesn't exist. The gap between you and people who've made things isn't talent. It's that they shipped. Err on the side of action. Decisions, contrary to what most people believe, are temporary. The person who acts, learns. The person who waits, wonders.
