# On Perspective

Life is about direction, not speed. There's an idea I keep coming back to: see distant things as if they were close, and close things as if they were distant. The first part means taking the long view seriously. Treating future consequences as real. Not discounting tomorrow just because today is loud. The second part is harder. It means stepping back from what feels all-consuming. Seeing the problem at its actual size. Not the size your anxiety inflated it to. Anxiety makes everything take on a dimension much bigger than it deserves. Every problem, once it gets inside your head, expands to fill all the available space. Every thought loops back to it. But that's not proportionate attention. That's obsession. And obsession distorts everything. Perspective is never about the facts. It's about the frame. Your next move matters more than your last mistake. The past is fixed. Done. You can't change it. But the future is still open. Learn what you need to learn from what happened. Then let it go. Your attention belongs to what you can still influence.
