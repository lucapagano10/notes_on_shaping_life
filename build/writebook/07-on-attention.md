# On Attention

What you pay attention to, expands. Most people never really choose. Their attention gets grabbed by whatever's loudest. Whatever's in front of them. And then ten years go by and they're like, wait, how did I end up here? I didn't choose this. But you did choose it. Just not consciously. You chose it with your attention, day after day. Every action is a vote for the type of person you want to become. You're casting votes all day long. Most people don't realize they're voting. We know this. But there's something about it that's hard to feel. Because the shift is so slow. You don't notice the erosion until years later, when you look up and think: wait, how did I end up here? Your attention gets more limited every year. And everything around you is engineered to steal it. Once you're distracted enough, you just assume that's what life feels like.
