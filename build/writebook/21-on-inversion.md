# On Inversion

Instead of asking how to succeed, ask how to fail. Then don't do that. There's this idea from Charlie Munger that I keep going back to. In algebra, it is often easier to solve a problem if you invert it. Life is the same. I don't want to know how to be smart. I want to know how to be stupid, so I can avoid it. Tell me where I'm going to die, so I will never go there. stupidities, and the brilliance takes care of itself. Most people spend all their energy chasing what to do. The few who get ahead spend just as much time thinking about what not to do. Antifragility is the no's. Think about it. Every commitment you say no to is energy you keep. Every distraction you avoid is attention you save. Every bad decision you sidestep is a crisis you never have to solve. The no's compound just like the yes's do, except they compound by subtraction. They make everything else lighter. The same principle works with problems. When something is stuck, instead of asking how do I fix this, ask what would make this worse. Then stop doing that thing. You'd be surprised how often the problem isn't that you need to add something. It's that you need to stop doing something. This connects to how I think about risk. The best bets are ones where the worst outcome is manageable and the best outcome is life changing. You don't find those bets by asking what's the most I can win. You find them by asking what's the most I can lose, and making sure you can survive it. Every potential future is just mental masturbation. What are you doing right now with what you have today? Antifragility is working with what you've got. When you look at your life through inversion, the questions change. Not what should I start, but what should I stop. Not what do I want, but what do I want to avoid. Not how do I win, but how do I make sure I don't lose in a way I can't recover from. It's a quieter way of thinking. Less exciting than big ambitions and bold moves. But it's where most of the real progress happens. Because the person who eliminates the stupid mistakes doesn't need to be a genius. They just need to still be standing when everyone else has knocked themselves out. Avoid the standard stupidities. The rest takes care of itself.
