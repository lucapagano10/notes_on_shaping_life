# On Tradeoffs

There is always a tradeoff in every decision. If you can't find it, look better. This took me a long time to learn. I used to think good decisions were the ones where you got everything you wanted. No downside, no sacrifice, no cost. That decision doesn't exist. Every yes is a no to something else. Every hour spent here is an hour not spent there. Every path you walk down means a hundred paths you didn't take. And the dangerous part isn't the tradeoff itself. It's not seeing it. The worst decisions I've made weren't bold bets that went wrong. They were decisions where I didn't see the cost until it was too late. I said yes to something without realizing what I was saying no to. I chased one thing and only afterwards noticed what I'd let go of. Easy choices, hard life. Hard choices, easy life. The person who avoids hard tradeoffs doesn't avoid the cost. They just pay it later, with interest. The person who stares the tradeoff in the face, sees it clearly,

and chooses anyway, they're the ones who end up where they want to be. You can't dance and play the music at the same time. That's not a limitation. That's just how it works. Pick one. Do it fully. Know what you're giving up. And don't pretend you're not giving anything up, because that's when it catches you. It's always better to be effective (choosing the right things) than to be efficient (doing them perfectly). Because efficiency on the wrong thing is the most elegant way to waste your life. Every decision has a price. Aim to know the price before buying into it.
