# On Commitment

You can do anything in life. But not everything. Optionality sounds so reasonable. Keep your options open. Stay flexible. Don't commit too soon. So you float. You dabble. You keep every door open while walking through none of them. Optionality promises freedom. But it delivers paralysis. Every door you refuse to close is a door you don't walk through. The more options you keep, the less you choose. And the less you choose, the less you become. Here's the cruel part. Those doors close anyway. They just close on you instead of behind you. Options have expiration dates. Uncommitted possibilities rot. You can do anything in life. But you cannot do everything. This is just arithmetic. Not philosophy. If something isn't a clear yes, it's a no. Nothing in between. The person who commits fully to one path goes further than the person who hedges across three. Not because they chose better. Because they chose at all. Saying no is saying no to one thing. Saying yes is saying no to a thousand things. That's not a cost. That's the point. The sacrifice is what makes commitment mean something. Easy yeses are worthless. I think about focus like juggling. If you juggle too many balls, some are going to drop. The mistake is thinking you get to choose which ones.

Your capacity for excellence is inversely proportional to the number of your commitments. You have so many ideas. More than enough to kill your focus. The danger is never running out of things to do. The danger is never going deep enough on any of them. When you stop treating your current opportunity as a stepping stone to something else and start treating it as the only one that matters, opportunity finds you. And here's the thing about competition. You escape it through authenticity. When you commit to your own weird path, you're not competing anymore. You're just doing your thing. There's no competition for being you. Freedom is not infinite options. Freedom is choosing, then going all the way.
